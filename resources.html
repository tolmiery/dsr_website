Types of Disabilities


There are four categories of disability accommodated by RPI:


  • ADHD
  • Learning Disabilities
  • Physical, Medical, and Sensory Disabilities
  • Psychological Disabilities

In order to get accommodations for ADHD and learning disabilities, students are required to submit test results from within the last three years.

Available Accommocations


The accommodations listed on RPI's website are:


  • Alternatively formatted materials
  • Housing accommodations, such as a single bedroom or a wheelchair-accessible room
  • Note-taking assistance

  • Testing accommodations - Common testing accommodations are:

    • 50% extra time
    • 100% extra time
    • Distraction-reduced alternate testing environment

Additional accommodations are included on the accommodations request form. These are:


  • Preferential seating
  • Priority Registration
  • Extended time for in-class assignments

In some cases, accommodations not listed above may be desired. These accommodations generally require more thorough documentation outlining their necessity. Previously, students have been able to obtain accommodations for accessible classrooms, flexible attendance due to chronic condition, and permission to have food and drink during lecture.

Requesting Accommocations


The steps for requesting initial accommodations are:


  1. Complete an accommodations request form.
  2. Acquire documentation of your disability from your doctor.
  3. Submit all documentation, along with your request form, to Disability Student Services. Claire Mahoney, the director of the DSS, is reachable at dss@rpi.edu or mahonc6@rpi.edu.
  4. Meet with Claire to discuss your accommodations and how to alert your professors.
  5. After meeting with Claire, she will send you an accommodations memo outlining your approved accommodations. Distribute your memo to your professors and meet with them to discuss logistics.
    • It's best practice to distribute your memo at the beginning of the semester. Those with testing accommodations should make sure to distribute their memos at least a week before their first exam.

Each academic year, you will be required to renew your accommodations. The process is:


  1. Complete the accommodations request form and note any changes to your previous accommodations.
  2. If your documentation has gone out-of-date since the last academic year, you may be asked to send up-to-date documentation. Likewise, you may need to submit new documentation to support new accommodations.
  3. Claire will send you an accommodations memo outlining your approved accommodations. The distribution process is the same as for an initial accommodations memo.
